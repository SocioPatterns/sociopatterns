Several results from the SocioPatterns project were presented at the 2011 <a href="https://www.netscisociety.net/events/netsci">Network Science Conference</a> in Budapest, Hungary.

Ciro Cattuto reported on simulations of realistic epidemic spreading phenomena using data from a SocioPatterns deployment. These results will soon appear in <a href="http://www.biomedcentral.com/bmcmed/">BMC Medicine</a>.

Alain Barrat presented the results of our paper <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jtbi.2010.11.033"><i>What's in a crowd? Analysis of face-to-face behavioral networks</i></a> published in the Journal of Theoretical Biology (2011).

Anna Machens discussed the interplay between the timescales of evolution of a dynamical network and the timescales of dynamical process taking place on the network. 

Wouter Van den Broeck discussed the making of <a href="/gallery/infectious-sociopatterns-poster/">SIXTY-NINE DAYS OF CLOSE ENCOUNTERS AT THE SCIENCE GALLERY</a> in <a href="https://arthist.net/archive/1374">Arts, Humanities, and Complex Networks</a>, the 2nd <a href="http://www.leonardo.info/">Leonardo</a> satellite symposium.
